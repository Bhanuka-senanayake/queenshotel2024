HISTORY OF QUEEN'S HOTEL KANDY  


Queens Hotel: A Timeless Legacy of Colonial Elegance and Sri Lankan Charm.


QUEEN'S HOTEL KANDY
 

The Queens Hotel in Kandy, Sri Lanka, is a historic landmark that traces its origins back to the British colonial era. Established in the early 19th century, the hotel was designed to cater to the needs of British officials and travelers exploring the island's central highlands. Its architecture, a blend of colonial and traditional Sri Lankan styles, features high ceilings, wide verandas, and elegant wooden finishes, reflecting the grandeur of the era. The hotel's strategic location in Kandy, once the last stronghold of the Sinhalese kings before British annexation, added to its prominence as a premier accommodation option. During British rule, the Queens Hotel became a hub for colonial administration and social gatherings, hosting various events and dignitaries. With Sri Lanka's independence in 1948, the hotel continued to serve as a prestigious venue, adapting to the changing cultural and political landscape while retaining its historical charm. In recent decades, the Queens Hotel has undergone several renovations to modernize its facilities, ensuring that it meets contemporary hospitality standards while preserving its historical essence. Today, it stands as a celebrated symbol of Kandy’s rich heritage, offering visitors a unique blend of colonial elegance and modern comfort, and continues to attract tourists keen on experiencing Sri Lanka’s cultural and historical tapestry. Its enduring legacy reflects the fusion of past and present, making it a cherished landmark in the heart of Kandy. 

The Queen’s Hotel, nestled in the picturesque city of Kandy, Sri Lanka, is steeped in history and elegance. Its story begins with the “Dullawe Walauwa,” a residence commissioned by King Sri Vickrama Rajasinha. Following British colonial rule, the building transformed into the “Stainton Hotel,” catering to British troops. In 1869, it emerged as the “Queens Hotel,” a beacon of luxury. The Kandy Hotels Company Limited took ownership in 1895, expanding and enhancing the property. The Queen of Hearts restaurant, the Royal Ball Room, and The Pub Royal evoke old-world charm. Lord Mountbatten himself frequented the hotel during his tenure. With 80 rooms, including suites, two restaurants, a coffee shop, and a souvenir store, the Queen’s Hotel remains an enduring symbol of Kandy’s past and present. Its outdoor pool invites relaxation, while the echoes of history resonate through its halls .
